Wavelength Division Multiplexer (WDM):

Summer 2024

Wavelength division multiplexers serve as the foundation for fast fiber-optic communication by allowing multiple data streams to be carried over a single fiber. Each data stream has it's own laser transmitter and these are sent to a optical multiplexer (thin-film filters). After exiting a single fiber, this single stream is demultiplexed through the same methods, and recieved on photodiodes.

Inspired by this, I built a 3-port WDM, using three laser pointers, two trichroic crystals, photodetectors, and arduinos. I created a custom communication protocol for the green laser, and utilized Arudino's Serial for communication with the other lasers, which allowed for at least 92% transmission accuracy across all lasers. We also printed on Fusion 360 stands to ensure alignment of the lasers and diodes. Additionally, we experimented with disturbances and data-recoovery code.

Polarized Double Slit Experiment:

Winter 2025

After learning about how light diffracts in the classical double slit experiment, I became fascinated in all the different properties of light. Building upon the double-slit experiment, I found this paper explaining the impact of polarization on the experiment. This project aimed to re-create this paper and measure the impact of different percentages of polarization.

I used a 650 nm diode laser as my light source, linear and variable polarizers, and a photo-diode connected to an Arduino for data recieval. Suprisingly, when the double was polairzed, result behaved like a single slit experiment because the the wave collapsed under observation. Additionally, I verified Malus's Law by measuring the intensity against the degree of polarization in the lienar polarizer.
